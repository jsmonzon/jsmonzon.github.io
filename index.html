Halo to halo variance using a semi-analytic framework

Acoording to the ΛCDM model of the Universe, galaxies form inside of dark matter haloes in a hierarchical manner (small to large). The mass functions and density profiles of these halos (and their substructer) can be used as a constraint on the nature of the dark matter particle. We aim to investigate and quantify the different sources of scatter that make up the variance in the Sub Halo Mass Function (SHMF) using a computationally-inexpensive semi-analytic model.

Metalicity measurements from calcium lines

Our MilkyWay (MW) hosts a population of dwarf galaxies and globular clusters which, due to their proximity, can be resolved into individual stars. These satellites are prime targets for investigations into galaxy formation, dark matter and cosmology but are often contaminated by foreground MW stars. Accurate measurements of metallicity (or [Fe/H]) can be powerful membership distinguishers such that we are using Keck/DEIMOS spectra to investigate the empirical relationship between [Fe/H] and the strength of the Ca II triplet absoption lines.

Abundaces in the Orion molecular cloud with SOFIA/EXES

The Becklin–Neugebauer/Kleinmann–Low (BN/KL) region in the center of the Orion molecular cloud is the best studied region associated with massive star formation at only ~400 pc away. The region is complex, hosting several distinct cloud components that trace different physical states of the nebula. We used high resolution (R~60,000) spectra from the Stratospheric Observatory for Infrared Astronomy (SOFIA) to make spatialyl-constrained abundace measurments on water and silicon emission lines.

effective opacity of the intergalacitc medium

The Intergalactic Medium (IGM) is a diffuse gas, mainly consisting of ionized hydrogen and helium, that permeates the space between galaxies in the large-scale cosmic web. We measured the effective opacity of the IGM (τ_eff) as a function of redshift, using the spectra of distant star forming galaxies from the COSMOS Lyman-Alpha Mapping And Tomographic Observations (CLAMATO) survey.
